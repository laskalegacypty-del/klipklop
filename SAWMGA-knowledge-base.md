## SECTION 2: RULEBOOK (January 2026)

### 2.1 Divisions / Age Categories

| Division | Age Range |
|----------|-----------|
| Child | 3–13 years |
| Junior | 14–17 years |
| Senior | 18–44 years |
| Veteran | 45+ years |

**Lead Line Rules:**
- Hurry Scurry only
- Poles must be flat
- Maximum speed: trot

### 2.2 Entry Requirements

**Dress:**
- Dress shirt with collar
- Long pants
- Boots: minimum 13mm heel, smooth sole (no gumboots)
- FEI-approved helmet mandatory at all times when mounted

**Tack Rules:**
- Shanked bits: maximum 100mm shank length
- Hackamore: maximum 130mm
- Martingales: permitted (with restrictions)
- No tongue straps
- No blinkers
- Crops: maximum 75cm with flap
- Spurs: limited depending on age category

**Horse Requirements:**
- Valid SAEF Horse Passport required
- Minimum age: 3 years
- No pregnant mares
- Yellow ribbon for stallions
- Red ribbon for kickers

**Penalty for dress violation:** 1 second

### 2.3 Competition Procedures

- Riders grouped approximately 5 per group
- 2 runs per event — best time counts
- Must enter arena within 1 minute of notification
- Judge signals white flag to start
- Maximum 120 seconds to complete course
- Re-rides only permitted for: timer malfunction or improperly set course

### 2.4 Penalties Summary

| Offence | Penalty |
|---------|---------|
| Dress violation | 1 second |
| Obstacle knocked down | 2 seconds each |
| Barrel knocked in Barrel Race | 5 seconds each |
| Exceeding 120 seconds | NT (No Time) |
| Loss of control | NT |
| Dismount | NT |
| Course failure | NT |
| Cruel treatment of horse | Disqualification |
| Bucket knocked in Fig 8 Flag | Disqualification |
| Hoof on Keyhole chalk line | Disqualification |

### 2.5 Judges

- Must be a licensed Senior SAWMGA Judge
- Apprentice judges must complete certification within 24 months
- Minimum 8 shows, including the National Championship
- Must pass arena test and written test
- Senior Judges: refresher course every 2 years

### 2.6 Equipment Specifications

| Equipment | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Timer | POLARIS Timer |
| Poles | 25mm PVC, 2m long, 100mm colour band at tip |
| Pole base | Standard black, 270mm diameter |
| Barrels | 210L unweighted plastic, max 940mm height, 600mm diameter |
| Flag buckets | 10L black, sand filled to 7cm from brim, 30cm cloth on 60cm dowel |
| Speedball cone | Large soft rubber pylon, 125mm opening, 60cm height, golf balls |
| Keyhole | 90° wings 15cm, chalk line 4cm wide, 3.05m radius circle |

### 2.7 Arena Requirements

- Minimum size: 37m × 76m
- Ideal size: 40m × 80m
- Running start: 24.4m
- Minimum 4.5m from obstacles to fence (Keyhole: 7.5m)

### 2.8 Individual Event Specifications

#### Barrel Race
- Cloverleaf pattern
- Barrel spacing: 18.3m and 27.5m
- Penalty: 5 seconds per barrel knocked

#### Big T
- 3 poles + 2 barrels
- Pole spacing: 6.4m apart
- Barrel spacing: 13.8m apart
- Penalty: 2 seconds per obstacle

#### Birangle
- 2 poles, same-direction turns
- Pole spacing: 13.8m apart, 45.75m total
- Penalty: 2 seconds per obstacle

#### Figure 8 Flag
- Reverse ride, exchange flags between 2 barrels
- Barrel spacing: 19.1m apart
- DQ for: dropping flag on ground or knocking bucket

#### Figure 8 Stake
- Reverse ride, 2 poles, opposite-direction turns
- Pole spacing: 19.1m apart
- Penalty: 2 seconds per pole

#### Hurry Scurry
- 3 jumps + 1 pole
- Jump heights: Level 0+1: 30cm; Level 2+: 45cm
- Obstacle spacing: 15.25m intervals
- Penalty: 2 seconds per obstacle
- DQ if all 3 jumps knocked down

#### Keyhole
- Run into circle, turn inside, return
- Circle: 6.1m diameter (3.05m radius)
- Distance to centre: 27.45m
- DQ if any hoof touches chalk line

#### Pole Bending I
- 6 poles, 6.1m spacing, 30.5m total
- Weave pattern
- Penalty: 2 seconds per pole

#### Pole Bending II
- 6 poles, 6.4m spacing, 38.43m total
- Different pattern
- DQ for breaking timing line

#### Quadrangle Stake
- 4 poles (2 on each side of timing line)
- Width: 22.88m, total distance: 48.8m
- Penalty: 2 seconds per pole

#### Speed Ball
- Single cone at 30.5m
- Drop golf ball into cone
- Penalty: 2 seconds for dislodging ball
- DQ if ball not in cone at finish

#### Speed Barrels
- 3 barrels in line at 18.25m and 18.3m spacing
- Weave pattern
- Penalty: 2 seconds per barrel

#### Single Stake
- 1 pole at 45.75m
- Penalty: 2 seconds

### 2.9 Team Events

| Event | Minimum Age | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| Rescue Race | 12 years | — |
| Ribbon Race | — | Crepe paper ribbon: 120cm × 2.5–3.8cm |
| Poles II Relay | — | 2–5 riders |
| Speedball Relay | — | 2–5 riders |

### 2.10 Protests

- Fee: R500 (refunded if protest is upheld)
- Judge's decision protest: within 30 minutes
- Scoresheet errors / conduct complaints: within 24 hours

### 2.11 Flag Signals (Annexure A)

| Signal | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| White flag raised | Course ready to start |
| White flag swung horizontally | Time penalty applied |
| Red flag swung low | No Time (NT) or Disqualification |

### 2.12 Warm-Up Arena Protocol (Annexure B)

- Pass left-shoulder to left-shoulder
- Inside track reserved for slower work
- Announce when overtaking
- No lunging in warm-up arena
- Red ribbon: kickers
- Yellow ribbon: stallions

## SECTION 3: SAWMGA CONSTITUTION (Amended 29 March 2021)

### 3.1 Overview

- **Full name:** South African Western Mounted Games Association (SAWMGA)
- **Status:** Voluntary association, national body recognised by SAEF
- **Jurisdiction:** Republic of South Africa
- **Headquarters:** Residential address of the duly elected Secretary (as determined by Council)
- **Non-profit:** Public benefit organisation (Section 30 Income Tax Act)
- **Founded on:** Human dignity, equality, non-racialism, non-sexism, freedom of association

### 3.2 Objects of SAWMGA (Summary)

SAWMGA exists to:
- Be the autonomous controlling and administrative body of Western Mounted Games in South Africa
- Direct, develop, promote and administer the sport
- Ensure non-racist and fair administration at all levels
- Promote sportsmanship and fair play
- Apply annually for SAEF membership and abide by SAEF rules
- Uphold and enforce SAWMGA rules and codes of conduct
- Encourage development and training of coaches and judges
- Select representative athletes for local and international competition
- Award local and international colours
- Recognise and comply with SAIDS, WADA, and Equine Anti-Doping regulations

### 3.3 Membership Structure

**Ordinary Members (9 provinces):**
1. Western Cape WMG
2. Gauteng WMG
3. Eastern Cape WMG
4. Free State WMG
5. Limpopo WMG
6. KwaZulu-Natal WMG
7. Northern Cape WMG
8. North West WMG
9. Mpumalanga WMG

**Special Members:** Bodies representing athletes, coaches, judges, and judicial committees

**Associate Members:** Bodies such as Defence Force, Police Force, Schools, Tertiary Education institutions

**Athletes:** Individual members; must be members of an Equestrian Club; annual membership renewable; subject to annual SAEF levy

**Honorary Life Members:** Persons recognised for notable service; may attend AGM and receive advance event notifications

**Patrons:** Persons appointed by Council; enjoy same privileges as Honorary Life Members (excluding voting)

### 3.4 SAWMGA Council Composition

- President
- Vice-President
- Treasurer
- Secretary
- 9 Provincial Presidents (one per province)
- Representatives of Special and Associate Members
- Honorary Members
- Portfolio holders (5–10, non-voting): Development & Transformation; Technical & Coaching; Judges & Officials; Finance & Administration; International Affairs; Venue & Schedules; Marketing & Advertising; Liaison, PR & Communications; Veterinary & AHS

**Council rules:**
- Minimum 2 women required on Council at all times
- President/VP/Treasurer/Secretary: elected every 3 years, max 6 consecutive years
- Council meets at least 2 times per year (plus AGM)
- Quorum: 50% + 1 of Council present
- Decisions by simple majority; President has casting vote on tie

### 3.5 Meetings

| Meeting Type | Notice Required | Quorum |
|-------------|----------------|--------|
| AGM | 45 days notice; agenda 21 days before | 50% + 1 |
| OGM (Ordinary) | 21 days notice | 50% + 1 |
| SGM (Special) | 14 days after requisition; held ≥21 days later | 50% + 1 |

- AGM held annually, within 4 months of financial year end (31 December)
- Chairperson: President (or VP in absence)
- Voting: show of hands (poll if 40%+ of members demand it)
- Constitution amendments require 75% majority vote with 21 days notice

### 3.6 Voting Rights

- President, VP, Treasurer, Secretary: 1 vote each
- Each Ordinary Member: 1 vote
- Each Special Member (excl. Judicial rep): 1 vote
- Each Associate Member: 1 vote
- Honorary Life Members: 1 vote
- Co-opted members: no vote
- Must be personally present (or by video conference) to vote

### 3.7 Financial Rules

- Financial year: 1 January – 31 December
- All payments require 2 authorised signatures
- Annual audited financial statements required
- Levies due to SAWMGA by 1 January each year
- No liability to be incurred beyond available funds

### 3.8 Discipline and Disputes

**National level disputes** (handled by Judicial Committee):
- Constitution interpretation disputes
- Council vs member disputes
- Disputes between members from different provinces
- Disputes arising during or after SAWMGA National events

**Provincial/club level disputes:** Handled internally; appeal to Judicial Committee after completion

**Dispute process:**
1. Submit written complaint to Council with R2,000 non-refundable admin fee (may be waived)
2. Respondent has 10 working days to respond in writing
3. Complainant has 5 working days to reply
4. Committee issues written decision
5. Either party may appeal to SAEF within 7 days of decision

**Penalties available:** Warnings, fines, suspension, banning from events, termination of membership

### 3.9 Dissolution

- Requires 75% majority vote at an SGM
- Remaining assets transferred to similar organisation at Members' discretion

## SECTION 4: SAEF CONSTITUTION (South African Equestrian Federation)
*(Amended and adopted: 12 July 2014)*

### 4.1 Overview

- **Full name:** South African Equestrian Federation (SAEF)
- **Status:** National controlling body of equestrian sport in South Africa; member of SASCOC
- **Non-profit:** Public benefit organisation
- **Colours:** Dark green, yellow and white
- **Financial year:** 1 January – 31 December

### 4.2 Structure

- **National Council:** Highest authority
- **Exco (Executive Board):** Handles day-to-day administration
  - Composition: President, Vice-President, Treasurer, Secretary General, 9 Provincial Presidents, 1 Athletes Commission rep
- **Terms:** 4 years; maximum 8 consecutive years as President or VP
- **Gender requirement:** Minimum 40% female members on Council and Exco

### 4.3 Ordinary Members (9 Provinces)

Eastern Cape, Free State, Gauteng, KwaZulu-Natal, Limpopo, Mpumalanga, Northern Cape, North West, Western Cape

### 4.4 SAWMGA's Relationship with SAEF

- SAWMGA is recognised by SAEF under Technical Committee clause 27.1.8.14 (SAEF Western Mounted Games)
- SAWMGA applies annually for SAEF membership
- SAWMGA must abide by SAEF Constitution, Rules and Regulations
- Disputes not resolved within SAWMGA may be escalated to SAEF

## SECTION 5: CODE OF CONDUCT FOR RIDERS AND MEMBERS

This Code applies to all riders, members, officials, and representatives participating in any SAWMGA-sanctioned event or activity.

### 5.1 General Behaviour
- Conduct yourself with honesty, integrity, and respect at all times
- Behaviour bringing SAWMGA or the sport into disrepute will not be tolerated
- Comply with all SAWMGA rules, policies, and the constitution
- Discrimination, bullying, harassment, or intimidation is strictly prohibited

### 5.2 Sportsmanship
- Show respect toward fellow competitors, officials, spectators, and organisers
- Accept decisions of judges and officials without argument
- Encourage fair play in victory and defeat
- Avoid abusive language, aggressive behaviour, or unsportsmanlike conduct

### 5.3 Horse Welfare
- The welfare of the horse is paramount and comes before competition results
- Ensure horses are fit, healthy, and properly prepared for competition
- Cruelty, neglect, or excessive use of aids will not be tolerated
- Proper care, feeding, and stabling must be maintained before, during, and after events

### 5.4 Safety
- Prioritise safety for yourself, your horse, and others at all times
- Helmets and appropriate riding gear must be worn as required by SAWMGA regulations
- Follow arena rules, marshalling instructions, and event safety protocols

### 5.5 Representation and Image
- Promote a positive image of the sport at all times
- Public comments, including on social media, must be respectful and not damaging to SAWMGA, officials, or other members
- Alcohol or substance abuse at events or while representing SAWMGA is strictly forbidden

### 5.6 Membership Responsibility
- Keep personal and club information up to date with SAWMGA
- Pay membership fees and event entries in full and on time
- Support the growth of the sport through volunteerism and positive involvement

### 5.7 Disciplinary Action
- Breaches of this Code may result in disciplinary action per the SAWMGA Constitution and Disciplinary Procedures
- Penalties may include: warnings, suspension, fines, or termination of membership

## SECTION 6: OVERCOUNT PRINCIPLE
*(Published: 10 February 2026)*

The overcount principle tracks how many levels above your current level you achieve in a qualifier.

**How it works:** For each game in a qualifier, if you achieve a time that places you in a level higher than the level you entered, the difference counts as an overcount. Overcounts across all games in a qualifier are totalled to determine your new level.

**Example table (5-game qualifier):**

| Level Entered | Game 1 Achieved | Game 2 Achieved | Game 3 Achieved | Game 4 Achieved | Game 5 Achieved | Total Overcount | New Level |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | 0 | 1 | 0 | 2 | 3 | 6 | 1 |
| 0 | 3 | 3 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 6 | 2 |
| 1 | 3 | 2 | 1 | 3 | 0 | 5 | 2 |
| 2 | 3 | 4 | 4 | 2 | 1 | 4 | 3 |
| 3 | 4 | 4 | 4 | 4 | 2 | 4 | 4 |
| 2 | 2 | 3 | 1 | 2 | 3 | 2 | 2 |

*(Note: The "overcount" per game = Level Achieved minus Level Entered, if positive; zero if not.)*

## SECTION 8: PROVINCIAL COLOURS CRITERIA 2026 (ALL PROVINCES)

### 8.1 Qualifying Shows

- Each province must hold **no less than 4** and **no more than 12** qualifying shows per year.

### 8.2 Eligibility for Selection

1. Only riders in good standing with their club, SAWMGA, and SAEF are eligible.
2. Riders must have competed with the **same horse-rider combination** in **no less than 11 of the 13** SAWMGA events to be eligible for team selection.
3. Riders must have competed in **at least 2 qualifiers** in the province where they are eligible for selection.
4. Riders may participate in as many horse-rider combinations and levels as they choose during annual qualifiers.
5. Best times per game are accumulated on a Provincial Combined Scoresheet throughout the year.
6. A rider qualifying for Nationals will be entered at National Levels according to their best times and the SAWMGA Rating Matrix.
7. **Level placement for Nationals:** If 8 or more of a combination's 13 event times place them in a higher level, they move up. If fewer than 8 times meet the current level, they move down.
8. Riders are ranked by **best overall scores across all 13 games**.
9. Double NT in a competed game = **200 seconds** awarded on the final scoresheet.
10. Game not entered = **NT** awarded on the final scoresheet.

### 8.3 Confederation Colours (Provincial Colours)

Age groups are based on age as of **1 January** of the applicable year:

| Age Group | Age Range |
|-----------|-----------|
| Children | 13 years and younger |
| Juniors | 14–17 years |
| Seniors | 18–44 years |
| Veterans | 45 years and older |

Confederation Colours awarded to:
- Top 6 Children Boys & Top 6 Children Girls
- Top 6 Junior Boys & Top 6 Junior Girls
- Top 6 Senior Men & Top 6 Senior Ladies
- Top 6 Veteran Men & Top 6 Veteran Ladies

All L4 combinations per age group and gender are placed on a scoresheet; points allocation equals the size of the group.

### 8.4 Association Colours (Provincial SAWMGA Colours)

- All L3 and L4 combinations not selected for Confederation Colours
- 10 best ranked L2 combinations per age group
- 5 best ranked L1 combinations per age group
- 5 best ranked L0 combinations per age group

### 8.5 National Championships Selection

| Category | Quota per Province |
|----------|--------------------|
| Lead Rein | Top 5 Children per province |
| Level 0 & 1 | Top 5 riders per age group per level |
| Level 2 | Top 10 riders per age group |
| Level 3 & 4 | All riders selected; compete in defined age groups |
| Disabled Riders | Selected as before; compete in lead class of their own |

### 8.6 Tie-Breaking

If tied for the last position in a team: the combination with the **highest score at the last show attended** takes preference. If still tied, the **highest aggregate score from the last 2 shows** takes preference.

### 8.7 Selectors

The selectors are the **SAWMGA Provincial Chairman, Vice Chairman, Secretary, and Treasurer** in office. They submit selected teams to the SAWMGA Management Committee for final approval and publication. Teams are published in order of their rankings.

Selectors may:
- Set a minimum competence/scoring standard for selection
- Place PDI riders (as per BBBEE legislation) for development purposes
- Consider exceptions: social restrictions, acts of nature, small/new provinces, logistical restrictions in large provinces

### 8.8 Important Rules

- **Selector decisions are final.** No correspondence will be entered into after teams are selected.
- **No substitutions** in teams after entries have closed.
- **No horse substitutions** at Nationals — selection is for the horse-rider combination, not the individual.
